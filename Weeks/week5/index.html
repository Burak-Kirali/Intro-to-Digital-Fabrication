Printing a 3-d Object
Scanning an Object

For my final project I had no idea till this weak. My friends told me his idea and invited me to the project. The aim of the project is to detect where on the hoop does a basketball hits. This may sound simple but after a little bit of research it's challenging. Firstly we have to find a way to detect where on the hoop does the ball hits. To do this, we thought of using force or presure sensors but their working principle is a little complicating. We don't need the exact measurement of the force but the readings on the sensors should force times a coefficeint so that we can locate where on the hoop the ball hit by the sensors readings. I searched online how can I do this, and I found that we have to use strain gauges. Strain gauges operate in the principle that when a force is aplied to a surface the metal comrpoess or strectches a little and the strain gauge(which basicly is a resistor) changes in length acordingly which causes changes in resistances(I'm confident in my knowledge till here). After here I guess we have to use a whitestone bridge and measure voltage and amplify it so that arduino can read it. However I feel I'm stuck in my research and utulize the 2 sensors lessons on week6 and week7 to fletch out this idea.
I feel that me and Demir have to a lot of testing this week to just understand how the ball hitting the hoop effects the sensors. Since our measurement will just be a number representing how much a small area on the hoop bent, finding the location may not be as simple selecting the sensor with the highest reading. Also if the bent is homogenous on the hoop we have to think of putting little independent metal pieces to measure the force. Another thing we may have to consider is that one part of the hoop is attached to it's holder while the part is not and this may also effect our measurements.
In the project we'll but a hoop and built our project on it. I don't know how many strain gauges we'll use but if it's more than 6 we have to think about how to read data from them because there only 6 analog pins on arduino. What I imagine for our project is that an arduino board collects data from the sensors and do some statistic to locate the place that the ball hit. Then this data is sent to a computer where a python program visualizes this(if we can't come to this part lighting the LED's to show where the ball hit is also enough)
