Burak's Failed Rotator Project

This week I was inspired by the square orbits, a visualization of fourie transforms, and wanted my kinetic sculpture to represent a square idea. The idea is that there's a rod that rotates and at the end of the rod another rod rotates and the path of a point in the outer rod around the center piece resembles that of an square. Here's the video of my inspiration.

Why I couldn't finish it

When I was planning my design I primarily focused on the gears and the how the 2 rotating rods will work. During testing those parts worked and my idea of transfering rotation to the edge by multiple gears also worked. However, for my design I needed a 2cm tall rod to stick out of the motor to make all the systems work and I had hard a time attacthing that rod to the motor. First I tried to attach it by heat-shrinks inspired by one of my classmates it broke 2-3 times then I decided to glue it which still didn't work. I was going to give up when someone suggested to tape it, and it worked. However it took me 3 hours to realize that tape was the option. I initially disregarded tape as I thought it wouldn't create a rigid connection. That's why I went with multiple ideas which all failed. Because I spent way more time on the rod then I had anticipated I couldn't finished my design, but I tested my gear mechanism and prooved that my idea worked. Also throughout my kinetic scultupture I always used M3 bolt mainly 20mm lonh but using some 30mm long ones to attach the support sturcture to the motor. I've chossen m3 because it was abundant in the lab and had a lot of nuts that I can use with.

In this video I show that I can transfer a rotation from the center gear to the most outer gear and a piece there can rotate. If had finished my design, the motor would turn the big piece I was holding. With this I would've achieved rotation on a already rotating piece.

This is the main design of my kinetic sculpture. There are 4 gears that carry the rotation from the center to edge. These gears have 18 teeths. To make the outer rod spin 3 times more compared the rest of the system I atteched a 6 teeth gear to the end and a rod on top of it. I'm using a wooden rod to rotate the main center gear and the outer gear. It has a diameter of 4.82mm and the 2 gears have designed for that. There's also a supoort structure to hold the wooden rod for the little gear. On the main rod you can soee 4 holes cutten exatcly for that. I had 3 gears that would transfer the rotation from center to the dge of the rod. I wanted them to rigedly attach to the rod while also allowing them to spin. To achieve I thought of making a big hole in them and putting a circular piece that I bolt on the main rod. You can see this circular piece on the video. It's a little hard to see it here. While I was designing the big rod I knew that I had to balance the weight of the gears on the opposite side to make it horizontal. The way I did was that I found a metal piece and drilled 2 holes in. Then I drilled 2 holes in the card-board piece. Since I did this after laser-cutting it's not shown the fusion design. I drilled 2 holes so that the metal piece would rotate. When it rotated it ofset the center of mass from the main axis and caused the rod to tilt. I tried to put this metal piece towards to edge of the rod and far away from the center so that it balanced better.

This is how I attached a rod to the motor. I found this wooden rod at the lab and cut it by wooden saw. Before taping it I thought that if I flatten the area over the rotating rod, the rod will rotate better(I had problem with the motor being in path of the rod). For that I cad-ed a piece to attach to the motor. Then I used 2, M3-30mm bolts to the attach it to the motor. The nuts I used only covered the top of the rod. To make tighten he wooden piece I attached the same piece cut in card-board(I first cut in card-board then laser to make sure my design was correct). With this only the tip of the bolt was hanging and the nut secured. If hadn't put the card-board the the bolt will be loose and it moved up and down.


There were 3 parts of the yellow that I had to consider. The places for the M3 bolts, and the position of a little yellow circle and the rotating handle of the motor. I measured the diameter and distance beetween bolt-holes and designed it accordingly. Instead of making a piece that fits perfectly into the yellow circle I decided to be redundant and made hole bigger then the circle and handle. Since I had redundancy, I made sure the the pieces were going through the hole. Besides that to not interfere with other parts of the motor so I made it not too big.

Design of the Individual Part Gears

In my kinetic sculpture I had 3 different gear-designs. The center gear, the 3 middle gear and the small gear. Both the center gear and the 3 middlegears have the same number of teeth and size, they only differ by the hole inside them as this holes is constrained by how to gears attach to the main spinning rod. For 3 middle-gears I've cut a big hole in them to put the support hole that kept the 20mm M3 bolts. The little gear had the 1/3 of the teeth of the other gears so that it would spin 3 times more. While I was making the gears I used the python version of nthe spur-gean add-on in Fusion360. Also in one of the tutorials I've watched. I've learned that pitch diameter is the place where 2 gears intersect. This allowed me to easily sketch the holes for main rotating part.

I don't have a specfic reason for many of the parameters chosen here. I've choosen 18 teeth so that the small gear still has plenty of gear. Also I wanted the distance beetween 2 gears to be 60mm and it happened to be 10/3 module of a 18 teeth gear. After creating the gear, I manually eddited the center hole to be 4.82mm. I achieved this with playing with the skecth files the programs has generated.

When I was designing the 3 middle gears, I wanted them to be the same size as the center gear(that's why I'm not putting their creation here I used the same numbers as I did in the center gear). Also I wanted the center gear to attach to the main rod without wobbling. I thought aboout a tube that goes in the gear and This tube is bolted the main rode. I made thigh margin with diameter's of the gear and the tube the gear. This allowed the gear to rotate while keeping in the same place To achieve this I made a 8mm diameter in the middle. The tube had a 3mm hole in it for the M3 bolt. To make the tube piece strong I made the hole in the gear

When I first cut the little hole it a kerf of 0.2mm so I cadded a piece with 8.2mm outer diameter os that it would become 7.8mm when cutted. I never measured the diameter after the design change, but it perfectly in the gear with little woogle.

Besides choosing the little gear I had no specific design choices. To make the size of the gears same with the other gears I've choosed a 10/30 module size. After that I changed the center circle's radius manually to 4.82mm so that the wooden rod can fit in.

Design of Rods and other Support Structures

To make the rods cool ,also less hazardous to me while testing, I rounded the edges of the rods by fillets. Besides that I desgined a piece to support the little rod. I origionally planned it to be directly underneath the main rod but to the available nuts it hanged a little under. This actually helped with the wooden rod as the hanging place allowed place for the wood to stay. On my original design there was a hole for the wooden-rod but then I thought it was unnecesary. However after I realized the hole would have secured the wooden-rod I didn't have enough time to re-print the part and also test the system as a whole

I had no specific requirements for the design of the little rod besides then it had to have a 4.82mm hole to support the wooden rod. I choose 8cm by 2cm size because I wanted the peice to be not small and big. While cad-ing it I had some problems with aplying fillets that's why I aplied it to the 2 sides seperately. Here's a photo of the fillet.

For the main-rotating piece I spaced 60mm beetween holes for the gear. For the holes of the 3gears I just aplied a rectangular pattern. I've given a 3.10mm diameter for the M3 bolts. I've choosed 3.10mm for some wiggle for the bolts but after printing them I realized it was a little too much. When I put the bolts the nuts secured them so I didn't consider changin the 3.10mm diameter. Also I spaced a 40mm for the space beetween the main gear and the little gear and placed hole for the support structure

The holes for the bolts are placed unsymetric to the hole for the small gear. I could've spaced them evenly but I wanted them to have a little distance that's why I placed them 18mm away. Also I tried to leave some distance beetween the edge of the main rod.

The support sturcutre is there to have a support for the wooden rod. On the assembly of all the parts, I put some holes on the main rotating piece and by rotating the gears in the assemvly I found places where the bolts didn't intefere with the gear. Then I just cadded a piece to that would be palced underneath it.
